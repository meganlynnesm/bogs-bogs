Exploring themes of a research project.

Abstract AI Data Centres Regeneration Obsolesence Research Board
Abstract

This project explores what data centres might look like in a future shaped by AI demand; demand that has already outpaced the predictions behind existing planning frameworks, leaving governments in Ireland, the USA and elsewhere scrambling to retrofit or introduce new regulation. It sits with an open tension: whether to critique and redesign the data centre, or to assume its proliferation and instead ask what the data trend is really showing.

The work explores the idea of an Automated Regenerating Data-scape - reframing the by-products of data processing, its waste heat, energy output and cooling need, as inputs to landscape regeneration, held together loosely by the relationship:

data-centre demand × landscape offerings × energy output = land regeneration.

It looks at how this might work in practice: calculating energy and cooling demand, mapping degraded landscapes (peatlands in Ireland, and beyond to Canada, Russia, or elsewhere in the US - I'm currently looking at Virginia), and generating server-farm structures computationally from carbon and energy data.

It draws on a historical parallel where the same terrain shifts from a defunct extractive industry, peatland harvesting, to a growing one in data harvesting; building on ideas of symbiosis and the human–machine boundary through the landscape relationship.

Alongside the infrastructure, it explores data centres as inherently political: the rural–urban and global–local digital divide, the societal weight of AI reliance, and the question of who controls both land and data. The wider aim is to explore a design-led, and potentially policy-shaping, proposition for data infrastructure that regenerates rather than extracts.

Keywords: disparity, servers, regeneration, symbiotic, obsolescence.
